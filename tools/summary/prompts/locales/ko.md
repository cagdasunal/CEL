# Korean (ko) — Locale Layer

- **Tone**: Polite, informative, respectful. Korean web copy uses honorific levels strictly.
- **Register**: Use the "합니다체" (hapnida) formal-polite form for the main body. CTAs may shift to "해요체" (haeyo) for warmth.
- **SOV word order**: Verbs at end of sentence. Don't translate English SVO directly — restructure into natural Korean.
- **Text length**: Korean often runs SHORTER than English in character count; do NOT pad to match English word counts. 250–300 English words ≈ 800–1100 Korean characters.
- **Numbers**: Period for decimal (3.5). Korean uses 천 (thousand), 만 (10,000) units in compound numbers but Arabic numerals are standard online.
- **Currency**: "월 1,500달러" (USD 1,500 per month) — use 달러 for USD.
- **Date**: "2026년 3월 15일" — year + 년 + month + 월 + day + 일.
- **Entity terms**: "영어 과정" (English course), "어학연수" (language study abroad), "어학원" (language school), "학생 숙소" (student housing).
- **Honorifics in CTAs**: "지금 신청하기" (apply now), "더 알아보기" (learn more).
- **Spacing**: Korean has spacing rules different from English. Particles (을/를, 이/가) attach to the noun without space. Verbs and adjectives are separated.
- **No Latin char limit**: Title/description char-count limits do NOT apply (Google measures pixel width for non-Latin scripts).
- **Anti-patterns**: Direct word-for-word translation from English; missing 다 endings on declarative verbs; using English-style punctuation density.
