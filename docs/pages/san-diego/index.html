Program Overview

Adult English Programs in Pacific Beach (16+) — Small Classes, Real Progress

CEL San Diego is a boutique English language school at 945 Hornblend Street in Pacific Beach — the only English school in the neighborhood, teaching English in San Diego since 1981. Choose from 10 English programs with an average of 7 students per class (maximum 12), start dates every Monday, and CEL shared apartments or homestay options.

We keep the school boutique on purpose: an average of 7 students per class, capped at 12, so every lesson includes real speaking time and direct feedback — not a lecture hall.

Most of our students stay between 4 and 24 weeks, though this varies significantly by nationality — some join for a short 1–2 week trip, others for a full academic year of 48+ weeks.

Why Pacific Beach

Why Learn English in Pacific Beach, San Diego?

Pacific Beach is one of the most walkable beach neighborhoods in San Diego — and CEL is the only English school here.

Walking Distance to the Beach

300m from the Pacific Ocean — about 3 blocks.

Garnet Avenue

Cafes, restaurants, and nightlife on the street right by our school.

Mission Bay & Crystal Pier

Flat water, boardwalk, and one of San Diego's oldest pleasure piers, both a short walk away.

Surf Culture

Pacific Beach is a genuine surf neighborhood, not a tourist backdrop — boards, lessons, and locals are part of daily life here.

La Jolla, 10 Minutes Away

Cliffs, sea lions, and upscale shopping, a short drive up the coast.

Balboa Park & Downtown, 15 Minutes Away

Museums, the San Diego Zoo, and the Gaslamp Quarter, easily reached — without living in the middle of it.

The California Lifestyle

Surf before or after class, sunset yoga on the beach, a BBQ evening by the pool at your shared apartment — in Pacific Beach this isn't a weekend excursion, it's the daily routine.

Year-Round Sunshine

San Diego's mild climate means outdoor class trips and beach time are realistic almost every week of the year.

Our Campus

A Boutique English School in the Heart of Pacific Beach

Our campus sits on Hornblend Street in Pacific Beach, not in Downtown San Diego. After class, students can walk to Garnet Avenue, cafés, shops, and the beach instead of planning the day around a long commute.

A beach-adjacent campus doesn't mean a lighter curriculum. CEL San Diego holds CEA accreditation for the maximum 10-year term, is authorized under SEVIS to issue F-1 student visas, and is a member of English USA — the same academic standards you'd expect from any accredited school, delivered from a genuinely walkable neighborhood instead of a downtown high-rise.

The CEL Method

How We Teach English at Our Pacific Beach Language School

Classes are built around speaking, doing, and using English actively — connected to real situations students experience in San Diego, from daily conversations to academic and professional communication.

All CEL teachers hold at least a bachelor's degree and recognized ESL qualifications such as TESOL, CELTA, or DELTA. Our academic program is overseen by Aaron Kilmer, Academic Director, who holds a master's in Teaching English as a Second Language and has 13+ years of experience in English language education.

Interactive, Real-World Lessons

Classes built around speaking, doing, and using English actively — connected to real situations in San Diego.

Small Classes, More Speaking Time

Classes average 7 students and are capped at 12 — more time to speak, more individual feedback, a group small enough to know each other.

Continuous Teacher Feedback

Teachers correct specific mistakes and give regular feedback on speaking, writing, pronunciation, fluency, and accuracy.

Progress Monitoring

Structured check-ins help you see what you've improved, what level you're working toward, and what to focus on next.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to study English at CEL San Diego?

+

Course prices start from US$300 per week, with accommodation from US$270 per week. Total cost depends on your course, length of stay, and accommodation choice. See our full cost breakdown for a detailed weekly and monthly estimate, or request a free personalized quote.

How large are the classes?

+

Classes average 7 students and are capped at 12. We publish this number openly so students know what to expect before they book.

Are CEL San Diego teachers native English speakers?

+

Yes. CEL San Diego teachers are native English speakers and qualified ESL teachers. All teachers hold at least a bachelor's degree, bring classroom experience with international students, and hold recognized ESL teaching qualifications such as TESOL, CELTA, DELTA, or equivalent credentials.

Where exactly is the school?

+

CEL San Diego is at 945 Hornblend Street, in Pacific Beach — about 300m (3 blocks) from the ocean, just a step away from Garnet Avenue. We are not in Downtown San Diego, which is roughly 15 minutes away by car.

What visa do I need?

+

If you study 24 or more lessons per week (18 clock hours), you'll need an F-1 student visa, and we'll issue your I-20 through SEVIS. If you study fewer than 24 lessons per week, ESTA or a visa waiver is typically sufficient, depending on your nationality.

What makes CEL San Diego different from other English schools in the city?

+

We're the only English school located in Pacific Beach — San Diego's other English schools and university programs are in Downtown and other parts of the city. We combine that with an openly published small class size (avg 7, max 12) and our own student apartments with a pool, jacuzzi, and fitness center.

Do you offer Business English in San Diego?

+

Yes — our English + Career Development program is CEL's Business English option in San Diego, built for career-focused students studying 2–24 weeks for professional advancement. See the course page for curriculum and schedules.

Can I combine English with surfing?

+

Yes — English + Surfing is one of our 10 programs, combining English lessons with surf instruction in the neighborhood where surf culture is part of everyday life, not an add-on excursion.

Where will I live?

+

Most students choose one of our shared apartments in Pacific Beach (Standard, Premium, or Superior), or a homestay with a local family. Premium and Superior include a pool, jacuzzi, and fitness center; Superior adds beach volleyball, tennis, and basketball courts.

Do I need to book through an agent, or can I enroll directly with CEL?

+

You can enroll directly with CEL San Diego. You receive the same course, written confirmation, accreditation-backed enrollment process, and student support as any CEL student, plus direct contact with a named advisor before arrival.

What does CEL stand for?

+

CEL stands for College of English Language. CEL San Diego is our Pacific Beach campus, teaching English in San Diego since 1981.
