Understanding Your Website Tracking

Last updated June 2, 2026

We set up tracking on your website so you can see how visitors use it and which actions matter most for your business. This guide explains what we measure and what each name means, in plain language. You do not need any technical background to read it.

If you ever open Google Analytics and see a word you don't recognize, come back to this page. It is here to save you a confused moment.

The short version

Every time someone visits your site, we quietly note the things that help you make decisions: which courses they look at, when they click Apply or Contact, how far they read, and where they came from. We never collect anything that would identify a person by name.

Think of it as a count of helpful moments, not a recording of individuals.

What we measure, grouped by what it's worth to you

The actions that matter most

These are the moments when a visitor does something with real business value. They are the numbers to watch.

  • Apply clicks. Someone clicked an Apply Now button. This is your strongest sign of interest.
  • Contact clicks. Someone clicked a Contact button to get in touch.
  • WhatsApp clicks. Someone clicked to message you on WhatsApp.
  • Form submissions. Someone sent a contact form, signed up to the newsletter, or booked a call.

Interest signals

These show what people are curious about, even before they reach out.

  • Which courses or accommodation options they browse.
  • Which specific course or room they click to learn more about.
  • When they open the full page for a course or a place to stay.
  • When they click through to read one of your blog articles.
  • Which offers and buttons they click.

Engagement

These show how involved people are once they land on a page.

  • How far down a page they read.
  • Which FAQ questions they open.
  • When they jump to a section using the on-page menu.
  • When they switch between tabbed content.
  • How they move around using your menu and footer.
  • When they switch the site into another language.

Visitors and traffic

The basics about who is visiting and where they came from: page views, visits, new visitors, and the source that brought them, such as a Google search, a direct visit, or a link from another site.

The event names, in plain English

Inside Google Analytics, each tracked moment has a short name. Some names are chosen by us to be clear. A few are Google's own standard names, which we keep because they switch on Google's built-in reports. Here is what each one means for your business.

Two words you will see a lot

An event is simply one tracked moment, like a single button click or one page view. When you see a big number next to an event, that is how many times it happened.

(not set) means the small detail behind a moment was not recorded, usually because the moment happened before we switched a particular measurement on. It is normal for older dates, and it clears itself for everything going forward. If you ever see it on recent days for something important, tell us and we will look into it.

What we do not track

Your visitors' privacy is protected by design.

  • We do not store anyone's name.
  • We never record payment or card details.
  • Email addresses and phone numbers are hidden before they reach the reports.
  • We follow your cookie banner. If a visitor declines, Google counts the visit in a private, cookie-free way that never identifies the person.
