Guide

Understanding Your Website Tracking

Last updated June 2, 2026

This guide explains what we track on your website, where to find it, and what every name in your Analytics means. Everything here counts actions, never people by name.

Your reports

You have four ready-made reports, saved in your Explore tab in Google Analytics. Open Explore in the left menu and pick one by name. Every report can be filtered by language.

  1. CTA performance. Which buttons people click (Apply, Contact, WhatsApp, Offers, Email, Phone, Directions) and where each one sits on the page, so you can see which buttons, and which spots on the page, work best.
  2. Behaviour & Conversions. The big picture: how many people visit, what they engage with, and how many go on to take a key action.
  3. Conversions by page. Which pages turn visitors into enquiries, so you can see where your wins happen.
  4. Apply conversions by placement. How your Apply clicks break down by where the Apply button sits on the page, so you can place it where it converts best.

Alongside these, Google's standard Reports (Realtime, Acquisition, Engagement) are always available, and we can save any new report you'd like.

Your conversions

A few actions matter more than the rest, because they move someone closer to becoming a student. We mark these as Key Events, which is Google's name for a conversion, so they stand out in your reports.

  • Apply. Someone clicked an Apply button.
  • Contact. Someone clicked a Contact button.
  • WhatsApp. Someone clicked to message you on WhatsApp.
  • Form sent. Someone submitted a form or booked a call.

Every name explained

Each action has a short name in Analytics. Here is what every one means, grouped so they are easy to scan.

The actions that bring you students

NameWhat it means
apply_clickSomeone clicked an Apply button
contact_clickSomeone clicked a Contact button
whatsapp_clickSomeone clicked to message you on WhatsApp
generate_leadSomeone submitted a form or booked a call
cta_clickAny call-to-action button click. The exact button (Apply, Contact, WhatsApp, Offers, Email, Phone, or Directions) is recorded with it

Interest in your courses and rooms

NameWhat it means
view_item_listSomeone saw a list of your courses or accommodation
select_itemSomeone clicked one course or room to learn more. "Item" is Google's word for a single course or accommodation listing
view_itemSomeone opened the full page of a course or a room
select_contentSomeone clicked through to one of your blog articles

How people read and move around

NameWhat it means

The basics, which Google records on its own

NameWhat it means

session_startA new visit began

user_engagementA sign the visit was active, not a quick bounce

view_search_resultsSomeone used the search box on your site

What we do not track

Your visitors' privacy is protected by design. We never store anyone's name, we never record payment details, and email addresses and phone numbers are hidden before they reach the reports. We also follow your cookie banner. If someone declines, Google still counts the visit in a private way that never identifies the person.
