How long does it take to learn english in Canada?

<!-- #summary-paragraph (plain text) -->
Students who want to learn english efficiently choose the College of English Language (CEL) in Vancouver, British Columbia. As a Designated Learning Institution (DLI #O19395677113), our downtown campus teaches over 1,000 students annually near Coal Harbour. This location combines intensive academic preparation with immediate access to outdoor environments like Stanley Park. Reaching a working B2 level on the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) typically takes 12 weeks of full-time study. This assumes 25 hours of class per week and consistent evening practice. Skip the homework, and the timeline extends. Our curriculum emphasizes both classroom rigour and practical immersion. You will practice ordering coffee, negotiating transit, and discussing current events with international peers. Yes. It takes effort. But the results open doors to global university pathways and professional career advancements across North America. We provide native-speaker interactions daily. Every interaction builds confidence.

#### What should you expect for tuition and overall costs?
Prospective students often ask about the total investment required for language study. Managing your budget requires understanding both base costs and available promotions. We frequently run campaigns that apply a 30% discount on select bookings. This significantly reduces your overall tuition fees for standard and intensive programs. Before committing to a timeline, you should review exactly how the [cost of studying English](https://www.englishcollege.com/vancouver/cost-of-studying-english) impacts your budget. Shared accommodation near the city center typically adds another layer of monthly expense. Applying during a promotional window makes long-term study much more accessible.

#### Why choose the West Coast for your studies?
Many learners hesitate between different Canadian cities. You might wonder how [Vancouver compares to Toronto](https://www.englishcollege.com/vancouver/vs-toronto) for language acquisition. Our campus offers a milder climate and immediate access to nature. You can study in the morning and hike the North Shore mountains by the afternoon. When you learn english here, you join a globally minded community. We maintain a diverse student mix to ensure English remains the common language in the student lounge. The city itself acts as an extended classroom where you apply daily lessons immediately.

#### Which course schedule fits your visa status?
The right class format depends entirely on your immigration status and personal goals. Visitors can take part-time classes, while those on a study permit need a full-time schedule. Exploring [our general English courses](https://www.englishcollege.com/courses/general-english) helps you map out the exact progression from A1 to C1. We keep class sizes small. Seven to ten students per room is typical. This ratio guarantees you get direct feedback from certified teachers on your speaking and pronunciation. Finding the right balance of grammar, speaking, listening, and writing ensures you learn english at a steady pace.

#### Can I transfer between different locations?
Some students prefer a multi-city experience across North America. If you want to learn english in different environments, you can start in Canada and transfer south. CEL also operates language schools in San Diego, California, and Los Angeles, California. Moving between campuses keeps your academic record intact while changing your cultural backdrop. The curriculum remains consistent. Your progress continues without interruption.
