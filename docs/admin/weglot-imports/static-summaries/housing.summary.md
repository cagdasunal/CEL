Find Your Space

<!-- #summary-title (plain text) -->
How to choose your new home in California for language studies?

<!-- #summary-paragraphs (rich text — two paragraphs, paste as Markdown) -->
Finding your new home in California or British Columbia begins the moment you book your classes. The College of English Language arranges accommodation for every student who enrolls, guaranteeing a comfortable arrival in San Diego, California, Los Angeles, California, or Vancouver. Across our three West Coast campuses, you choose between living with a local host family, renting a room in a shared apartment, or joining a student-only residence managed directly by CEL. Securing your housing alongside your courses aligns your start date on a single calendar, simplifying visa application timelines. Students arriving from Brazil, Switzerland, and Japan frequently highlight the convenience of having airport pickup, contract paperwork, and move-in logistics handled by our dedicated student services team. Choosing an officially managed placement guarantees quality standards backed by the Commission on English Language Program Accreditation (CEA). Picking the right living arrangement sets the foundation for your study abroad experience. Your new home in California awaits.

A well-chosen student housing arrangement places your new home in California just a short distance from the classroom. Our support team manages every detail of your stay, allowing you to focus on your studies and explore your host city. When you choose to [study English in the USA](https://www.englishcollege.com/learn-english-usa), living near the campus means less time commuting and more time building relationships with classmates from around the world. Yes. Convenience matters.

#### Where are the shared apartments and residences located?
In San Diego, students stay in Pacific Beach. This is the exact same beachfront neighborhood where our classrooms sit roughly three blocks from the sand. You can select your new home in California from Standard, Premium, or Superior building tiers. The [Superior Pacific Beach apartment](https://www.englishcollege.com/pb/cel-shared-apartment-superior-pacific-beach) offers fantastic amenities like a swimming pool, jacuzzi, volleyball courts, and barbecue areas. Students like Timeo from Belgium lived at Ava apartments, noting the spacious rooms and easy walk to supermarkets. Our Los Angeles housing sits beside the partner school near Santa Monica Beach, placing you a ten to fifteen minute walk from the classroom. In Canada, accommodations cluster around the downtown core and Gastown. Students can reach class, transit options, and the seawall without needing a car. Discover more about the city side of life on our [Vancouver campus page](https://www.englishcollege.com/vancouver).

#### What makes a host family a good option?
Treating a homestay as your new home in California provides an immersive environment for practicing english after class. Homestays suit students who want everyday speaking practice and a structured local routine. Sharing meals with your hosts introduces you to the culture firsthand and accelerates your language progress. Many students build long-term relationships with their experienced host families. Whether you prefer a standard room or a [premium homestay arrangement](https://www.englishcollege.com/pb/premium-homestay) with a private bathroom, this option ensures a supportive environment. Daily conversations at the dinner table help you build confidence speaking english naturally. You get to experience the local lifestyle authentically while receiving helpful tips on getting around the city. The daily interaction makes a massive difference.

#### How does living with other students shape your time abroad?
Choosing a shared student apartment or a CEL-managed residence maximizes your social opportunities. New students arrive every Tuesday, so you are constantly surrounded by fresh faces. Living together with other students makes it incredibly easy to make friends from different countries. Our students frequently mention meeting peers from Switzerland, South Korea, France, and Brazil in their daily routines. You will cook meals together, plan weekend trips, and share a diverse cultural exchange. This shared environment transforms your new home in California into a lively social hub. You can combine language learning with the thrill of catching waves if you take our [English plus Surfing program](https://www.englishcollege.com/courses/english-plus-surfing) and head to the beach with your roommates. After school, many students catch the sunset and surf the incredible waves in San Diego. The friendships you build in your residence often last long after your classes end.

#### Can you change your accommodation type later?
Flexibility remains a core part of our student services. You might book a homestay for your first four weeks and then transition into a shared apartment. This approach allows you to adjust your new home in California as you become more familiar with the city and meet potential roommates. Our team handles any change requests after move-in, ensuring you always feel secure. If you join a longer schedule, like our [academic pathway preparation](https://www.englishcollege.com/courses/global-pathway-preparation), having the ability to switch housing styles keeps your experience fresh. Many long-term students appreciate experiencing both a family homestay and an independent student residence during their stay. Options matter. You are never locked into a single choice for an entire year if your needs evolve.

#### What amenities support your daily routine?
Every CEL accommodation includes the essentials you need for a comfortable stay. Cleaning services are provided by CEL to maintain high standards across our properties. When you step out of your new home in California to head to class, you know you will return to a well-maintained space. You can expect reliable internet, furnished rooms, and access to laundry facilities. Students living in the shared apartments often organize social gatherings at the building amenities. Taking advantage of the shared pools or gyms creates a balanced lifestyle. A comfortable living space directly supports your academic focus, allowing you to [practice reading skills](https://www.englishcollege.com/post/5-language-skills-4-types-reading-become-fluent) without worrying about household maintenance. Finding the perfect new home in California allows you to truly relax and enjoy your time abroad.
