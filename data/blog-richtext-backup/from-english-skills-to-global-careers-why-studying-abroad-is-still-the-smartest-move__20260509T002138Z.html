Why Global Employers Value International Experience

Across industries — business, tech, hospitality, aviation, design, science — global hiring increasingly focuses on a shared set of competencies:

  • clear, confident communication in English
  • cross-cultural collaboration
  • initiative and independence
  • critical thinking under pressure
  • adaptability in unfamiliar environments

International experience strengthens all of these skills at once.

What’s more, speaking English in a native environment rewires how you process language. You stop translating. You start thinking in English. That’s what employers actually look for: usable English, not memorized grammar.

The Moment English “Clicks”

For Emilia from Italy, speaking up in the first week at CEL San Diego felt intimidating. She understood her classmates but worried about making mistakes.

One afternoon, she joined a small group walking to Pacific Beach after class. They talked about weekend plans, hobbies, and food. Emilia suddenly realized she wasn’t translating anymore — she was participating, laughing, sharing opinions in English.
It wasn’t dramatic, but it was powerful.
“In that moment,” she said, “English stopped being a subject. It became part of me.”This shift is one of the biggest study abroad benefits — and it stays with you your entire career.
