21 Business English Phrases That Open Doors

Below are 21 powerful phrases grouped by real professional situations — plus examples of when to use them.

1️⃣ Meetings: Lead and Participate with Confidence

  1. “Let’s get started.”
     Use it to open a meeting or online call naturally — confident but friendly.
  2. “Could you walk me through that?”
     Polite way to ask for clarification and show genuine interest.
  3. “From my perspective…”
     Expresses your opinion respectfully; perfect for international teamwork.
  4. “I see your point, but…”
     A diplomatic way to disagree and keep the tone positive.
  5. “We can revisit that point later.” (modernized from “circle back”)
     Shows structure and focus — ideal for keeping discussions on track.

2️⃣ Networking: Build Authentic Professional Relationships

  1. “What brings you to this event?”
     Smooth conversation starter at conferences or business meetups.
  2. “I’ve heard great things about your company.”
     Friendly compliment that opens dialogue.
  3. “I enjoyed our conversation — let’s stay connected.”
     Professional yet personal way to end a chat.
  4. “Can I connect with you on LinkedIn?”
     Reflects modern networking practice — confident and natural.
  5. “Let’s keep in touch.”
     Classic phrase for maintaining relationships after first contact.

3️⃣ Presentations: Engage, Guide, and Impress

  1. “Let me give you a quick overview.”
     Signals structure at the start of your presentation.
  2. “Let’s take a quick look at this chart.”
     Smooth way to draw attention to visuals.
  3. “To sum up, our main goal is…”
     Keeps your presentation clear and audience-focused.
  4. “Any questions before we move on?”
     Encourages interaction and shows confidence.
  5. “Thank you for your attention — I look forward to your feedback.”
     A professional, positive closing that invites discussion.

4️⃣ Collaboration & Teamwork: Everyday Communication at Work

  1. “Let’s brainstorm some ideas.”
     Encourages creativity and inclusion in group settings.
  2. “Can we arrange a short meeting to align?” (modernized from “catch-up”)
     Professional phrasing for quick team updates.
  3. “I’ll take care of that.”
     Shows initiative — builds credibility and trust.
  4. “Thanks for the update.”
     Simple, polite acknowledgment that keeps communication smooth.

5️⃣ Problem-Solving & Feedback: Stay Calm, Positive, and Clear

  1. “Let’s look at possible solutions.”
     Keeps focus on progress, not blame.
  2. “I appreciate your feedback — it helps me improve.”
     Demonstrates openness and professionalism — always well-received.

How to Practice These Phrases Every Day

  • In class: Repeat phrases in roleplays or mock meetings.
  • After class: Use them when ordering, networking, or volunteering.
  • In your journal: Write short reflections using 3–4 of these phrases daily. Over time, they’ll become part of how you think — not just what you study.
