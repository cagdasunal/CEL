HOW CAN I PRACTICE MY ENGLISH PRONUNCIATION?

Pronunciation is one of the most challenging aspects of learning English. Just look at words such as: “though,” “through,” “and ought.” They look like they’re pronounced similarly, but actually, they are not. To get them right, you’ll need to refer to a dictionary.

So how can you tame this beast called “English pronunciation”? Well, it takes time, devotion, and a lot of hard work. First, take a look at the following tips.

LISTEN AS MUCH AS POSSIBLE

Exposure to authentic speech is one of the most fruitful ways to hone your pronunciation. You can watch TV shows, listen to podcasts and audiobooks, and converse with native speakers at every opportunity.

Try to notice how people speak and how they shape their mouths. Also, pay attention to their intonation and how they connect words in sentences.

PRACTICE SHADOWING

Shadowing is a fun exercise. It includes listening to a phrase or a short sentence and then trying to repeat it in precisely the same way.

While you’re shadowing, pay attention to word stress, sentence intonation, the position of your tongue, and how your mouth moves.

RECORD YOURSELF

After you’ve been shadowing for a while, you can start recording yourself to see how you actually sound.

You can videotape yourself while uttering short phrases, describing a picture, or retelling a story. Then, listen to your recordings and analyze your pronunciation. If you notice some irregularities, take note and work on a specific sound/sound cluster.

MEMORIZE THE PHONEMIC CHART

Learning the International Phonetic Alphabet can be useful as dictionaries use the phonetic transcription of words.

USE A DICTIONARY

When you learn a new word, look it up in a monolingual dictionary to check its pronunciation. Also, many online dictionaries offer audio recordings of each term. You can even choose if you want to hear American or British English accents in some.

This can be particularly helpful if you can’t decipher the phonetic transcription or are unsure how to stress the word correctly.

PRACTICE A LOT

Unfortunately, there is no shortcut to mastering English pronunciation. There is nothing except practice. Sometimes mother-tongue interference hinders our progress because our mouths or tongues aren’t accustomed to specific positions.

That’s why you sometimes need to train your mouth and tongue to move in new ways. You can do this by observing how people talk and practicing in front of a mirror. There are phonetic books that describe how each sound is made, and you might get one and practice it within the comfort of your home.
